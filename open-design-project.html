Organic Robotics Lab MIT CSAIL Boston Centerless Open Design Project About

For the Open Design Project, my group created a suction-based organizer called SPLASH designed to keep users’ shower essentials secure and easily accessible. With french door mechanisms and drainage holes, it offers both function and style for any bathroom.

Over the course of the semester, my team completed a full product design cycle. I took a leadership role within the team: I divided and assigned tasks for members of our team, kept us on track for deadlines, and assembled the final report.

We began by brainstorming a wide range of ideas before ultimately deciding to make a new type of shower caddy. We conducted extensive user research, including interviews with potential users, to better understand their needs. Based on our findings, we developed an initial design and created a cardboard prototype to identify potential issues and areas for improvement. We then modeled a fully functional prototype using Fusion 360 and sourced components like hinges and suction cups from McMaster-Carr. After testing the prototype, we improved the hinge design to enhance stability and security. Our project won Best Presentation at the end-of-semester showcase.
